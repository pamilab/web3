DEVELOPMENT OF 2.4 GHZ ISM BAND UNSYNCHRONIZED STFT FMCW-GPR SYSTEM

An advanced 2.4 GHz FMCW radar system that can reliably detect underground objects by means of instantaneous signal processing and self-directed scanning has been developed. The entire process of concept, design, testing, and results represents practical engineering for real-world deployment.

About the System

Ground-penetrating radar systems are widely used in civil engineering, subsurface utility mapping, archaeology, and safety inspections to locate subsurface objects without digging. However, many commercial GPR devices are expensive, bulky, and consume more power, limiting their use in academia, small entities, and research applications.

To mitigate these constraints, this work presents a low-cost, portable FMCW GPR prototype assembled from commercial off-the-shelf RF modules, open software tools, and a motorized scanning platform. Operating in the 2.4 GHz ISM band, the system is suitable for shallow subsurface detection.

This system adopts FMCW technology for stable signal transmission and increased noise tolerance compared to impulse-based radar. Reflected signals are processed using STFT techniques with SDR digitization to visualize subsurface objects and estimate depth.

Why This Radar Matters

Reliable Subsurface Detection

Clear radar data to pinpoint buried objects and measure depth reliably — without bulky hardware or huge cost.

Portable and Easy to Deploy
