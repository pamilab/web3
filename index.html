FMCW Ground Penetrating Radar Prototype

An advanced 2.4 GHz FMCW radar system that reliably detects underground objects through instantaneous signal processing and self-directed scanning. Designed and tested as a real-world engineering solution.

Why This Radar Matters

Reliable Subsurface Detection

Produces clear radar data to accurately locate buried objects and estimate depth without bulky hardware or excessive cost.

Portable and Easy to Deploy

Compact design suitable for field testing, academic research, infrastructure inspection, and disaster response.

Validated Results

Tested under controlled conditions with measurement errors below 2%, ensuring confidence across different depths.

About the System

  • Low-cost, portable 2.4 GHz FMCW-GPR
  • Uses STFT signal processing and SDR digitization
  • Designed for practical, scalable research and real-world applications

Mission & Vision

Mission: Democratize subsurface detection with a reliable, cost-effective, and portable radar system for research, engineering, and safety.

Vision: Become a benchmark solution for accessible radar sensing, empowering universities, agencies, and industries.

The Problem & Our Approach

Ground Penetrating Radar is critical for civil engineering, disaster response, and underground mapping. However, existing systems are often expensive, bulky, and difficult to customize.

Engineering Philosophy

  • Stage-by-stage development
  • Validation at every step
  • Design-first, test-early approach
  • Hardware–software co-design
  • Focus on repeatability and real-world deployment

How the System Works

  1. FMCW chirp is transmitted to the ground
  2. Reflections return from subsurface objects
  3. Signals are mixed to obtain beat frequency
  4. Data is digitized using SDR
  5. STFT extracts depth-related components
  6. Results are visualized as radargrams

Building the Prototype

The system was developed through structured stages including concept planning, radar modeling, hardware integration, software development, and controlled testing.

Performance & Results

  • Mean error at 29 cm depth: 1.433%
  • Mean error at 72 cm depth: 0.863%

Waterfall and heatmap-style radargrams clearly show object presence, depth, and lateral position.

Applications & Growth

  • Civil infrastructure inspection
  • Underground utility mapping
  • Academic and laboratory research
  • Disaster search and rescue
  • Smart city subsurface assessment

The modular design allows future upgrades such as higher bandwidth operation, advanced signal processing, machine-learning classification, wireless data transmission, and autonomous navigation.
