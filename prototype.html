Building the Prototype

Concept and Planning

The project began by identifying limitations in existing GPR designs and selecting FMCW radar as the optimal solution. System requirements, operating frequency, and compliance constraints were defined.

Design and Simulation

Radar equations, chirp characteristics, and depth-to-frequency relationships were analyzed to predict system performance before hardware construction.

Hardware Integration

RF modules, control units, and digitization hardware were assembled into a unified radar front-end. Mechanical mounting improved stability and ensured repeatable testing conditions.

Software Development

Signal acquisition and processing were implemented using GNU Radio and Python, enabling real-time visualization and automated data handling.

Testing and Experimental Validation

The system was evaluated using controlled experiments with metallic targets embedded at known depths under a wooden platform.

  • Operating frequency: 2.4 GHz ISM band
  • Target depths: 29 cm and 72 cm
  • Multiple trials per depth
  • Manual tape measurements as reference

System Demonstration

Accuracy Performance

  • Mean error at 29 cm: 1.433%
  • Mean error at 72 cm: 0.863%

Processed data are displayed as waterfall and heatmap-style radargrams, enabling intuitive interpretation of subsurface features.
